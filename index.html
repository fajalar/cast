Cast a Spell!

(Shhh…it’s actually meditation. Surprise!) You probably already know that meditation can be good for you. You've probably been told a hundred times by friends, psychiatrists, and more importantly, random social media wellness influencers! Okay, maybe not “more importantly”... Perhaps you even downloaded an app that has a soothing voice (and a monthly fee), opened it twice, and let it get lost in the sea of app icons on your phone. Big same, as The Kids™ used to say.

Spell Meditation is a trick I'm playing on myself. And now I’m playing it on you. While you’re casting your spell, what you're actually doing is breathing slowly and paying attention to one thing for a moment. The spell’s on you, in a way. It’s also, more or less, meditation.1

How it works

  • Hold. Press and hold (or click) anywhere on the sigil. It starts to draw itself.
  • Breathe. Follow the light as it swells and settles. It's meant to help you pace your breath.
  • Release. Hold long enough and the spell lands. Let go early and it doesn't. No harm, no scolding. Try casting another spell.

Why this?

I created it because the “cast a spell” framing felt fun. And because I wanted to know something: will people sit still for one minute when "meditate for five" never quite does? Turns out the answer, for me at least, is yes.

And, plus side, there’s no account to make. No streak to protect. No notification nagging you to come back. It runs on (hopefully) any browser, it costs nothing, and it forgets you the moment you close the tab. Not that you aren’t great. That’s just a nice way of saying this site doesn’t use long-term cookies.
